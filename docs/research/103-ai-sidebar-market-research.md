# AI Sidebar Market Research (2026-03)

> Browser-based AI sidebar tools: features, pricing, user sentiment, and market dynamics.

---

## Product-by-Product Analysis

### 1. Sider (sider.ai)

**What it is:** Multi-model AI sidebar for Chrome/Edge. 5M+ downloads, 4.92 stars (111K+ reviews) on Chrome Web Store. The most popular in the category.

**AI Features:**
- Access to 20+ AI models (GPT-4o, Claude 3.5 Sonnet, Gemini 1.5 Pro, etc.) from a single sidebar
- Context-aware page Q&A: highlight text and ask AI to summarize, explain, translate, or respond
- YouTube video summarization
- Deep Research Agent: scans 100+ sources, generates cited reports
- Translation (50+ languages)
- Writing assistance (rewrite, expand, tone adjustment)
- "Wisebase" personal knowledge base: save chats, clips, sources, and reports in a searchable library

**Web Page Interaction:** Reads current page content. Users can highlight text for instant AI actions. Sidebar stays open alongside any webpage.

**Pricing:**
| Tier | Price | Credits |
|------|-------|---------|
| Free | $0 | 30 basic/day, limited features |
| Basic | $8.30/mo | 3,600 basic + 100 advanced/mo |
| Pro | ~$16/mo | More credits, Wisebase |
| Unlimited | ~$25/mo | Unlimited basic, high advanced |

**BYOK:** No evidence of bring-your-own-key support.

**Knowledge Persistence:**
- Chat history auto-syncs across devices (cannot be disabled)
- Star important chats for quick access
- Wisebase: dedicated knowledge base for saving and searching AI outputs
- Export conversations available since v5.4.0

**What Users Love:**
- Multi-model access without switching tabs
- Fast, reliable performance
- Deep Research produces genuinely useful cited reports
- Cross-device sync "just works"

**What Users Complain About:**
- "Unlimited" plans still have hidden credit caps; users report arbitrary quota reductions
- Sidebar feels cluttered with too many features crammed in
- Customer support is unresponsive; users report being ghosted
- Some AI outputs are broken or incomplete ("pretends to work but produces garbage")
- Desktop apps (Win/Mac) lack basic features like document upload

### 2. Monica (monica.im)

**What it is:** All-in-one AI Chrome assistant. Multi-model access with heavy emphasis on browser-native integration. 4.8/5 rating on Chrome Web Store.

**AI Features:**
- GPT-4, Claude, Gemini access in browser
- Text highlight instant AI responses
- YouTube / PDF / image / video analysis
- "Browser Operator" for automated web tasks (agentic)
- Deep Research with one-click reports
- Slides generation
- AI image and video creation
- Writing assistance across any text field
- Translation
- Custom "Skills" (user-defined AI workflows)

**Web Page Interaction:** Inline text actions on highlight. Browser Operator can navigate and interact with pages autonomously. Summarizes open pages, PDFs, and YouTube videos.

**Pricing:**
| Tier | Price | Access |
|------|-------|--------|
| Free | $0 | Daily limits (~30 queries) |
| Pro | $8.30/mo ($99/yr) | 5,000 daily basic, 200/mo advanced |
| Unlimited | $39/mo | Removes most limits (still soft caps) |

**BYOK:** Not supported. Monica has its own API platform (platform.monica.im) but this is for developers consuming Monica's API, not bringing external keys into the extension.

**Knowledge Persistence:**
- **Memo**: Personal AI knowledge base. Auto-saves YouTube summaries, PDF summaries, web summaries
- All conversations auto-saved with AI-generated titles
- Keyword search across conversation history
- "Continue Chat" on saved memo items for follow-up Q&A
- Customizable auto-save rules via notepad settings

**What Users Love:**
- Fits into workflow without replacing it
- Browser-native integration is best-in-class
- Memo knowledge base builds up over time, making AI more useful
- Custom Skills let power users automate repetitive prompts

**What Users Complain About:**
- Pricing is confusing; "Pro" still hits limits by Tuesday each week
- 50+ features create "analysis paralysis" and uneven quality
- Core AI chat is limited compared to direct ChatGPT/Claude subscriptions
- Bugs since Q3 2024: AI sometimes gives same answer to all prompts
- Suspected continued charging after cancellation
- "Half the features are gimmicks I'll never use"

### 3. Merlin (getmerlin.in)

**What it is:** 26-in-1 AI Chrome extension. Strong focus on content creation, social media, and email integration. 4.8 stars on Chrome Web Store.

**AI Features:**
- Multi-model (GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, others)
- Chat with webpage content, PDFs
- Summarize YouTube videos, articles, documents
- Content creation: personalized replies, social media posts
- 128-language support
- Platform integrations: Gmail, LinkedIn, Facebook, X (Twitter)
- "Projects" knowledge base with semantic search + vector retrieval
- Custom chatbots trained on your documents

**Web Page Interaction:** Reads page content for Q&A. Direct integration with Gmail (draft replies), LinkedIn (write posts/comments), and X. Summarizes any open page.

**Pricing:**
| Tier | Price | Credits |
|------|-------|---------|
| Free | $0 | 102 daily queries (generous) |
| AppSumo Tier 1 | $79 one-time | 5,000/mo |
| AppSumo Tier 2 | $149 one-time | 12,000/mo |
| AppSumo Tier 3 | $279 one-time | 20,000/mo |
| Regular subscription | ~$15-20/mo | Varies |

Note: AppSumo lifetime deals are a significant go-to-market strategy for Merlin.

**BYOK:** Under consideration as a Pro feature. Users have requested BYOK for models beyond OpenAI and for local models (Ollama), but not yet shipped.

**Knowledge Persistence:**
- Chat history syncs across devices (one account, multiple devices)
- "Projects" feature: upload documents, links, resources as reusable knowledge bases
- Train dedicated bots on Notion, Google Drive, S3 buckets, or URL lists
- Vector-based retrieval + semantic search

**What Users Love:**
- Generous free tier (102 daily queries)
- Lifetime deal on AppSumo is great value
- Social media integrations (LinkedIn, Gmail) save real time
- Projects/knowledge base is powerful for repeated research

**What Users Complain About:**
- UI can feel clunky
- Summaries occasionally miss key points
- Organizing chat history is difficult (feature request pending)
- BYOK not yet available

### 4. MaxAI (maxai.me)

**What it is:** "1-click AI anywhere" Chrome extension. Multi-model sidebar with emphasis on reading and writing speed. 1M+ users.

**AI Features:**
- Sidebar Q&A and task hub
- Multi-model: GPT-4, Claude 3.5+, Gemini 2.0, DeepSeek, Mistral, Grok
- YouTube summarization with transcript interaction
- Translation (58 languages)
- AI Vision (image interaction)
- Writing assistance (rewrite, expand, tone)
- Run multiple AIs simultaneously for comparison
- AI web search

**Web Page Interaction:** Page-aware sidebar for Q&A. Inline text actions on selection. YouTube transcript processing. Image analysis via AI Vision.

**Pricing:**
| Tier | Price | Access |
|------|-------|--------|
| Free | $0 | Basic features, fast AI |
| Pro | ~$10/mo | More limits |
| Elite | $20/mo | Unlimited content chats, unlimited fast AI |

**BYOK:** No clear evidence of BYOK support in search results.

**Knowledge Persistence:**
- Limited information on chat history persistence
- No prominent knowledge base feature found
- Focus is on in-the-moment AI assistance rather than building a knowledge library

**What Users Love:**
- Broad model selection (most models of any sidebar)
- Free tier is usable
- Fast parsing and response times
- Side-by-side model comparison is unique

**What Users Complain About:**
- Billing friction: forced annual subscriptions, cancellation difficulties
- Customer support ignores cancellation and refund requests
- Interface has become increasingly confusing over time
- Features have disappeared without notice (DALL-E, Stable Diffusion)
- Privacy concerns: extension requests broad browser permissions
- Adds 2.3 seconds of processing time to every page visit (performance penalty)
- Security: broad tab access could track browsing habits

### 5. Elmo (elmo.chat)

**What it is:** Lightweight, free AI sidebar by Lepton AI. Focused on summarization. No account required.

**AI Features:**
- Website summarization
- YouTube video summarization (including Bilibili)
- PDF chat
- Google Docs summarization
- Q&A from webpages
- Keyword exploration (fetches web info without navigating)
- Translation
- Basic mind maps

**Web Page Interaction:** Reads current page for summarization and Q&A. Processes YouTube transcripts. Handles PDFs directly.

**Pricing:**
| Tier | Price | Access |
|------|-------|--------|
| Free | $0 | All features, unlimited |

Completely free. No account needed. No API key needed. Powered by Lepton AI's own LLMs.

**BYOK:** Not supported. Users frequently request the ability to use custom APIs or choose models.

**Knowledge Persistence:**
- **None.** Closing the side panel erases all previous questions and answers
- No chat history, no saved conversations, no knowledge base
- Users must re-ask the same questions if they close the panel
- This is the #1 user complaint

**What Users Love:**
- Completely free with no strings attached
- No account required; instant use
- Fast (summaries in under 5 seconds)
- Privacy-focused: no data saved
- Simple, clean interface (does one thing well)

**What Users Complain About:**
- Zero persistence: closing the panel loses everything
- YouTube summaries fail when subtitles aren't available
- Frequent rate-limit errors and instability
- Cannot choose models or use custom API keys
- Chrome only; doesn't work in Arc, Opera, or other Chromium browsers
- No way to export or save results

## Category-Level Analysis

### What's Table Stakes (2026)

Every credible AI sidebar must have these to be taken seriously:

1. **Page-aware Q&A** -- sidebar reads current page, answers questions about it
2. **Multi-model access** -- at minimum GPT-4 + Claude + Gemini
3. **Text selection actions** -- highlight text, get instant summarize/translate/explain
4. **YouTube summarization** -- extract key points from video transcripts
5. **PDF chat** -- upload or open PDFs, ask questions
6. **Writing assistance** -- rewrite, expand, change tone in any text field
7. **Translation** -- at least 50+ languages

### What Differentiates (Emerging)

Features that separate leaders from followers:

1. **Knowledge persistence / personal knowledge base** -- Sider's Wisebase and Monica's Memo are significant differentiators. Most users cite "losing context" as a top frustration
2. **Agentic capabilities** -- Monica's Browser Operator and Chrome's native Auto Browse represent the next frontier. Multi-step autonomous web tasks
3. **Deep Research** -- multi-source cited reports (Sider, Monica). Going beyond single-page summarization
4. **Custom AI workflows** -- Monica's custom Skills, Merlin's Projects with trained bots. Let power users build reusable AI pipelines
5. **Cross-device sync** -- seamless history across phone/tablet/desktop
6. **Side-by-side model comparison** -- MaxAI's multi-model simultaneous execution

### What Nobody Does Well Yet

1. **True knowledge management** -- all "knowledge bases" are flat lists of saved chats/clips. None offer structured organization, tagging, linking, or graph-based retrieval
2. **BYOK** -- surprisingly absent from the sidebar category. Users request it everywhere but no major player ships it
3. **Conversation-to-notes pipeline** -- AI conversations generate insights, but there's no good way to distill them into persistent, organized notes
4. **Integration with existing PKM tools** -- no sidebar connects meaningfully with Notion, Obsidian, Roam, or Tana
5. **Privacy-preserving local AI** -- Elmo's "no data saved" approach is popular but comes at the cost of zero persistence

### The Chrome Native Threat

Google's January 2026 Chrome update is the biggest category disruptor:

- **Persistent Gemini sidebar** built into Chrome (not an extension)
- **Auto Browse**: agentic multi-step web tasks (research, booking, form-filling)
- **Personal intelligence**: connected to Gmail, Search, YouTube, Google Photos -- no extension can match this data access
- **Cross-tab context groups**: Gemini understands multiple related tabs as one research session
- **Gemini Nano**: on-device model for instant, private responses

This directly threatens every third-party AI sidebar's core value proposition. Extensions that only offer "chat with AI in a sidebar" are at existential risk. Survivors will need to differentiate on:
- Knowledge accumulation over time (Chrome Gemini is session-based)
- Multi-model choice (Chrome only offers Gemini)
- Domain-specific workflows (Chrome is general-purpose)
- User data ownership and portability

### Pricing Patterns

| Model | Examples | Observation |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| Free + subscription | Sider, Monica, MaxAI | Most common. Free tier is a funnel |
| Lifetime deal | Merlin (AppSumo) | Attracts budget-conscious users, limits recurring revenue |
| Completely free | Elmo | Sustainable only with own inference (Lepton AI) |
| BYOK | None in category | Massive gap. Every coding tool supports it, no sidebar does |

Universal complaint: "Unlimited" never means unlimited. Every paid tier has soft caps, and users feel deceived.

### User Sentiment Summary

**What delights users:**
- Instant AI access without leaving the current page
- Multi-model freedom (not locked to one provider)
- YouTube/PDF summarization saves real time
- When knowledge bases work well, they become indispensable

**What infuriates users:**
- Misleading pricing and hidden limits
- Poor customer support, especially around billing
- Feature bloat that makes the core experience worse
- Security/privacy concerns (52% of AI extensions collect user data)
- Losing context when closing the sidebar
- No way to organize or retrieve past AI interactions

The market is large but facing commoditization pressure from Chrome's native Gemini integration.

## Key Takeaways for soma

1. **The gap is knowledge, not AI.** Every sidebar does chat + summarize + translate. Nobody does structured knowledge management well. Soma's node-based data model is a genuine differentiator -- AI conversations that produce structured, linked, searchable knowledge rather than flat chat logs.

2. **Persistence is the #1 pain point.** Users across every product complain about losing context. Soma's design -- where everything is a node, persisted in a CRDT document -- directly addresses the category's biggest unmet need.

3. **BYOK is an unserved market.** No major sidebar offers bring-your-own-key. For power users and privacy-conscious users, this is a significant draw. Soma could offer BYOK alongside managed AI.

4. **Chrome native AI creates urgency.** Generic "AI sidebar" value is being absorbed by Chrome itself. Soma's differentiation must be clearly about knowledge accumulation and organization, not just AI access.

5. **Feature bloat is a cautionary tale.** Monica (50+ features) and Sider (cluttered sidebar) show that more features != better product. Users consistently prefer tools that do fewer things well.

6. **The read-think-write loop is underserved.** Users browse, read, think, and want to capture insights. Current sidebars treat each page visit as isolated. None support the cumulative knowledge workflow that soma's "think where you read" vision targets.
